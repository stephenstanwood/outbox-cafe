About the VeggeTamer 3000

You have tried the rest. Now meet the best. The VeggeTamer 3000 is the result of three years of kitchen research, fourteen prototype revisions, and one very frank conversation with a home economist from Dayton, Ohio. The conclusion was unanimous: American kitchens needed a device that could produce a perfect 3/8-inch diagonal medallion from a carrot — every single time — without guesswork, without waste, and without the quiet dread that comes from working with an ordinary knife.

The secret is the patented TameLock™ Guide Channel — a precision-machined aluminum rail that holds your vegetable at exactly 22.5 degrees while the EasyGlide™ Cutter Arm travels its fixed, firm path. You do not aim. You do not measure. You place, you pull, and you achieve perfection.

Three thickness settings on the VeggeGauge™ Dial — 3/8 inch, 1/2 inch, and 5/8 inch — cover the full range of serious vegetable work. Whether you're preparing a Sunday pot roast, a church-supper casserole, or simply getting a head start on the week, the VeggeTamer 3000 handles carrots, zucchini, yellow squash, and parsnips with identical, unhurried confidence.

  • Aircraft-grade aluminum alloy body — built for daily use, decade after decade
  • Surgical-steel VeggeSlice™ blade — dishwasher safe, rust-proof, stays sharp
  • Ergonomic VeggeGrip™ handle — walnut-finish molded resin, right-hand optimized
  • TameLock™ Guide Channel holds vegetables at fixed 22.5° diagonal angle
  • VeggeGauge™ Dial: three thickness settings (3/8", 1/2", 5/8")
  • Non-slip rubber feet — stable on wet or dry countertops
  • Includes 12-page VeggeGuide™ Instruction Booklet with 44 recipe suggestions
  • Includes CleanReach™ Brush for blade-channel maintenance between uses
Please note: The VeggeTamer 3000 is optimized for vegetables with a diameter between 3/4 inch and 1-3/4 inches. Use of beets will void the warranty. Potatoes, onions, and turnips are outside the TameLock™ operating specification. Harrington's assumes no liability for attempts to tame beets.
