Welcome, friend! :)

You have found the official community board for our one and only beloved Cloud #44-B, lovingly nicknamed Mr. Puff. He has been hanging above the Pebble Falls water tower for approximately ever, and we are SO glad to know him.

Please help yourself to a complimentary butterscotch from the tin in the vestibule (honor system — Doris counts).

— Doris & the welcoming committee

About our cloud

.--. .--. .( ).---.( ). (___.__)____(______) · · ·

Type: a small, well-behaved cumulus.
Mood: reliably pleasant.
Favorite snack: warm afternoon air rising off the bakery.

— portrait by Henry, age 8

How to spot him

If a single, cheerful, slightly lopsided puff is suspended above the water tower and the rest of the sky is doing its own quiet business — congratulations, you have located our friend!

Wave gently. Smile broadly. He prefers a little decorum but not too much.

— field guide, vol. 1

Sign up to wave!

Our weekly Tuesday Wave is going strong! Volunteers gather on the Pebble Falls footbridge at half-past noon and wave up at Mr. Puff for exactly four minutes.

Bring a friend! Bring a sandwich! Bring your excellent attitude!

— Marvelle, social chair

What's New on the Board! ✿

  • Tuesday after lunch Mr. Puff was photographed by Henry's grandmother and we all agreed he looked especially round and content. Wonderful! 📷
  • Sunday morning A first-time visitor named Pip stopped by the footbridge and reported feeling "tremendously cheered up." We are absolutely thrilled for Pip!
  • Right after the potluck Doris's butterscotch tin was found to be precisely as full as she left it. The honor system continues to honor itself!
  • Late last week A passing breeze briefly nudged Mr. Puff six (6) feet east. He returned to his usual spot of his own accord and we did not panic, not even a little.
  • Just this morning Marvelle baked thirty-six (36) cumulus-shaped shortbread cookies for the next Wave. Please take only two (2) so everyone gets some!
  • Earlier this season Our cloud achieved a thirty-one-day streak of being visible from the public library window. A new society record! ☆
