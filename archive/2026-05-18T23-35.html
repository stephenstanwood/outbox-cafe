Zenith Radio Corp. · 1953 Brochure Excerpt

Found tucked behind the Henderson shop bulletin board.

"Every Zenith leaves Chicago with one promise: when you turn the knob, the picture will be there. We do not merely manufacture television receivers. We manufacture the moment a family in Sandusky and a family in Pocatello sit down at the same minute to watch the same thing, and feel — for the length of one program — that the country has a shared living room."

From a customer letter, the Maitland family of Cedar Hollow:

"When Walt got our Zenith going Tuesday night, my husband called the children in from the porch one by one. We turned out the lamps. The screen warmed up the way bread warms up in the oven, slow and even. When the announcer said 'Good evening,' my youngest, Patsy, said 'Good evening' back, very polite, like he was in the room with us. None of us laughed. We just sat there in the glow until the program finished. I keep thinking about that — how he was polite to the announcer. That's the kind of thing a television does that I wasn't ready for."
