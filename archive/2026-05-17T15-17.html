How A Match Works (For The Tourist)

  1. The Stoop. Five or six steps, painted town-hall green, lined with hand-quilted runners by Mrs. Eulalie Drake.
  2. The Skip. Contestants begin at the top with both boots side-by-side. On the bell, they cascade — never two feet on the same step. Bonus points for syncopation.
  3. The Penalty. Touching the rail with a hand earns a chiding from the judge and one bag of peanuts forfeited to the crowd.
  4. The Tuesday Rule. Matches held on Tuesdays count toward the Society standings. Sunday matches are "for fun and pie."
  5. The Bow. Always bow to the porch. Maudine says the porch remembers, and Maudine has never been wrong about a porch.
