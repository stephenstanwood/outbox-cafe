The Parade · As It Actually Happened

Saturday, May 12 · 9:00 AM sharp · Linden Avenue, Cinderbrook · BREEZY

  1. Cinderbrook Senior Center Kazoo Brigade — gave it everything. Esther B. clapped both times they passed.
  2. Pickett & Sons Heating & Cooling — banner only this year. Their float (the "Comfortable Cloud") is in the shop.
  3. The Wandering Sons of Saint Avis — eleven members, four hymns, two complete strangers, in formation throughout.
  4. Cub Pack #181 — modified for low turnout. Marched in a tight rectangle. Den Mother very proud.
  5. The Wagon — empty this year on purpose. The Wagon represents possibility.
  6. Mayor Eldon Pim, atop a horse named Carl — both waved continuously.
  7. St. Avis Junior High Marching Band — TWENTY-NINE STRONG. Played "Stars & Stripes Forever" twice for the parking meter.
  8. The Float Formerly Known As "Welcome Spring" — now welcomes whatever season we're in. Don't ask. The crepe has been re-pulled too many times.
  9. Honeydew Floral Arrangement Float — sponsored by Honeydew Floral. Smelled incredible.
  10. The Cinderbrook Quilters Guild, pushing a cart — quilts visible from all four sides. Cart squeaked rhythmically. Possibly intentional.
  11. Big Earl from Big Earl's — walked in front of his own sign. Waved at the squirrel.
  12. The Junior Vocational Auxiliary — sang one song. It was lovely. Nobody is sure what it was.
  13. A Surprise Guest — turned out to be the wind.
  14. The Sweepers — always last, always proud. Carried EVERYTHING.
Total units: 14. Total minutes: 41. Total attendees: 4 humans, 2 dogs, 1 squirrel (strictly observational).

A Word from Mayor Pim

Folks. Folks. I want to be honest with you, in the way only a small-town mayor on a late-night corkboard can: the parade was GREAT. The parade was, in my professional opinion as the elected face of this town, our best one yet.

Did people come? Friend, that is not the question. The QUESTION is — did the parade happen? And I am here, on Channel 13, at two-fourteen in the morning, to tell you: YES IT DID. It happened SO HARD. It happened along the WHOLE ROUTE. It happened in a SLIGHT BREEZE. It happened with FOURTEEN UNITS and a HORSE and a WAGON, and if a parade happens on Linden Avenue and four people see it, you'd BETTER BELIEVE THAT'S STILL A PARADE.

Next year. NEXT YEAR. Same route. Same kazoos. Possibly a second horse. Mark your calendars. We'll be here. We are always here.

— Mayor Eldon Pim
