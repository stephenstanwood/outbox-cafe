Album & Trading Ledger of Wilbur L. Hartwell, Esq. · Begun Anno 1890 · 14 Linden Row, Providence, R.I.

Five winters now I have kept this album, and it gladdens me anew each evening I open it under the green-shade lamp. The chromolithographs of Messrs. Allen & Ginter, Goodwin, Duke, and Sweet Caporal are no idle trifles — they are pocket-sized triumphs of the printer's craft, a dozen lithographic stones or more for a single card, gilt-edged and richly inked, equal to any colour-plate in any gentleman's library. Click any card to read its full description; mark those acquired with a tick, star those still sought.

Trading Ledger

Every exchange I have made by post, set down with the care it deserves. The fraternity of collectors is the truest network of friendship I know — men and women I have never laid eyes upon, who pack each card in tissue and ribbon as though it were their own first-born.

From the Private Folio

Tipped-in among the album's back leaves — an advertising flyer kept for its colour, and a letter never sent.

Advertising flyer · Sweet Caporal Cigarettes · ca. 1894. A grand chromolithograph nearly eighteen inches across: a young woman in cream silk leans against a marble pedestal, her gloved hand lifting a slender white cigarette toward her lips. Behind her, peacock feathers fan out in metallic blue-green ink that shifts in the lamplight; the brand name arcs across the top in gold-foil capitals two inches tall. Beneath, in smaller letter — "THE STANDARD FOR LADIES AND GENTLEMEN OF DISCRIMINATING TASTE — Kinney Tobacco Co., New York." A border of laurel and tobacco leaves frames the whole. The gilt catches the lamp even reduced to flyer-size.
My dear Phineas —

The card has come. I held it under the green-shade lamp for the better part of an hour and could not quite believe my eyes. Allen & Ginter's World's Beauties, Series 1 — Mrs. Langtry, in the condition you described and finer. The gilt edges sing. I have wanted this card since I was sixteen years old, when I saw one in Mr. Pendragon's window in Newport and could not afford to ask the price. I stood at that window for half an hour. I remember the rain.

I shall not forget what you did for me. The bird cards are on their way to you by Wednesday's post, all twelve, wrapped each twice in tissue as you asked, with green ribbon at the outside — Miss Munn's method, which has not failed me yet.

Yours in earnest fraternity,
Wilbur
