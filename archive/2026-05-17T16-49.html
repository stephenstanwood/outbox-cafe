Joseph Priestley (1733–1804) is mostly famous for discovering oxygen. but in 1770 he picked up a lump of natural rubber — a strange gum from the Amazon — and rubbed it against a pencil mark, and the mark went away. he wrote about it in a small note. he named the material "rubber" for what it could do. then he went back to thinking about air. he did not know he had also invented the feeling of taking something back.
