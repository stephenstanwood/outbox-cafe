★ WELCOME, VISITOR ★ (i am so glad you found us)

Hello, hello — please come in, you are very welcome here, this is the public sign-in screen for the Museum of Lost Keys, which is located in the back half of the old Pellinore Building on Tremaine Street, between the dry-cleaner and the storefront that used to sell ribbon. I should mention, in the interest of being upfront, that this is a BBS frontage and not a real-time chat-line — it just looks like one — so if you type something and nothing happens for a while it is not because I am ignoring you, it is because I am probably upstairs sorting the new arrivals and have not heard the little bell over the door.

A small note before you sign in: we have had concerns expressed by our regulars about how nervous this opening screen sounds, and I want to say, in my own defense, that the museum has been broken into exactly once (1996, a person looking for a missing house key of their own, nothing taken, they apologized in a long letter) and ever since then I have been a little jumpy about who is on the other end of the wire. I am working on it. Please be patient with me.

★ MAIN MENU ★

Okay you are inside. The front room is the part with the velvet rope and the donation jar (a mason jar, donated, used to hold pickles) and the smell of furniture polish. Please pick a number and click. If anything looks broken it is probably broken — I am the only one who knows HTML here and I learned it from a paperback called The Friendly Web which has a smiling spider on the cover.

  1. BROWSE THE VITRINES
    9 cabinets, 411 keys, sorted by the room each key thinks it goes to. Yes I know that is unscientific, I am sorry, the previous system had us crying every Sunday.
  2. SUBMIT A KEY YOU FOUND
    You can mail it (PO Box 14, Pellinore Station) or drop it through the slot in the door. The slot is the one painted pink, not the green one. The green one goes into the wall cavity and we have never gotten it open.
  3. READ THE CURATOR'S WEEKLY ANXIETIES
    This is mostly me apologizing for things and then listing the new acquisitions. Updated Tuesdays. Sometimes Wednesdays if Tuesday got away from me, which Tuesdays will do.
  4. VISITING HOURS & HOW TO FIND US
    We are open afternoons. We are closed during weather. Please call ahead if you are coming from out of town because I sometimes forget to flip the sign and people knock and knock and it breaks my heart a little.
  5. SIGN THE GUESTBOOK
    Some patrons sign anonymously and some patrons draw a picture of the key they are still looking for. Both are okay, both are encouraged, neither is required.
  6. CHAT WITH OTHER PATRONS [offline]
    This was on the original specification but we never got it to work and honestly I am a little relieved.

→ Vitrine Index (top of mind)

  1. VITRINE 1 — The Brass Family. 71 brass keys, most house-shaped, one mystery (curved, possibly a piano). The mystery one arrived in a fish-and-chips wrapper, no return address, postmarked Astoria.
  2. VITRINE 2 — Bike U-Locks & Their Companions. 58 keys; 9 are matched pairs found on the same morning on different blocks, which I find either beautiful or upsetting depending on the day.
  3. VITRINE 3 — Tiny Diary Keys. 22 brass keys with little bows. We do not have the diaries. We are not going to open any diaries. That is not what we do here.
  4. VITRINE 4 — Filing Cabinet Keys (Cherished, Unclaimed). 44.
  5. VITRINE 5 — Hotel & Motel. 38, mostly plastic fobs from the Coast.
  6. VITRINE 6 — Skeleton Keys (Probably Decorative). 31. We label these "Probably Decorative" because the genuinely antique ones make people nervous and we get enough nervous around here.
  7. VITRINE 7 — Mailbox Keys. 27. If yours is here please come get it. Please.
  8. VITRINE 8 — Keys Whose Function Is Unknown. 64. The big one. Please feel free to peer in but do not tap the glass, the glass is on a hairline ledger of trust with the rest of the building.
  9. VITRINE 9 — Sentimental Loans. 56 keys on indefinite loan from people who do not want them back but also cannot throw them away. Honestly I get it.

→ How to Submit a Found Key

Pop it in an envelope (no padding required, keys are tough), include a small note with: WHERE you found it, WHEN, and any small detail about the weather or the mood of the street that day. We don't need your name. We love the weather notes. Someone once wrote "the air smelled like a copy machine" and I have thought about that sentence at least once a week for two years.

→ This Week's Curator Anxieties (week of 5/17)

  • The new fluorescent in Vitrine 4 is a touch too green and I worry it makes the brass look sad.
  • Someone left a key on the donation jar with no note and I do not know which vitrine it belongs to, I have been calling it Provisional Wesley.
  • The slot in the door has been making a sound like a small kazoo on windy mornings and I cannot decide if I love it or hate it.
  • I think we are getting a goose? A patron is donating a ceramic goose to hold the umbrellas. I am trying to be a person who can accept a ceramic goose.

→ Guestbook

The physical guestbook is the green ledger near the door, please use the attached pen on the short string. The string is short on purpose — the pen used to wander, and one time it wandered into Vitrine 8, and we do not talk about Vitrine 8 incidents lightly.

One more thing — if you came here because of a specific key you lost, you can click here to ask the resident mascot to take a look. She cannot really do anything but she likes to feel useful and I think we should all support that in each other.
