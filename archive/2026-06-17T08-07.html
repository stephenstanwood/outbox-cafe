From the foreword to the visitor's pamphlet

The conservatory was opened in 1979 by Mr. Imrich Lark, a former bicycle mechanic who had, by his own admission, been collecting bells "since a confused afternoon in 1948" and could no longer keep them in his kitchen. The room above the framing shop seats nine and contains, at last count, two hundred and eleven bells. Only twelve are out at any given time. The others rest in cigar boxes lined with felt that Mr. Lark cuts himself.

Visitors are encouraged to ring. It is the considered view of the conservatory that a silent bell is a sad bell, and that the loudest collection is the most respected.

The conservatory does not charge admission, but a jar by the door accepts a penny, a button, or a note about a bell you once loved.

The conservatory will reopen Tuesday, weather permitting and Mr. Lark willing. — Sound made nicely is a small public good.

Visitors who linger may consult the back room, where past arrangements rest under glass.
